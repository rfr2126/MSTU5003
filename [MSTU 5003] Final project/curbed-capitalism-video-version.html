CURBED CAPITALISM

a film by Cacau Araujo

About the film

"There's no free lunch" is one of the main adages of capitalism, but in high-cost New York City, one might find by the curb a free dining table set to enjoy their meals. Could the most expensive city in the US be an oasis of free stuff? Through random street encounters and by following professional "stoopers", this film explores the unspoken codes of stooping and an informal economy emerging from it.

ABOUT CACAU ARAUJO

Cacau Araujo is a Brazilian storyteller based in New York. As a writer and a filmmaker in the making, she has experience developing non-fiction audiovisual projects and campaigning for impact films. Currently, she is a graduate student at the Documentary Media Studies program at The New School, where she wants to further explore and uncover her authorship as a filmmaker.
